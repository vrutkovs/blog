Artificial Intelligence & Society 
* [SCOTUS Declines to Hear LLM-Backed AI Case Regarding Copyright](https://sfconservancy.org/blog/2026/mar/04/scotus-deny-cert-dc-circuit-thaler-appeal-llm-ai/)
  
The U.S. Supreme Court has declined to hear *Thaler v. Perlmutter*, letting stand a DC Circuit ruling that works generated entirely by AI without human creative input are not copyrightable. The decision provides some relief to the free and open-source software community, as it primarily applies to purely machine-generated images and avoids complicating existing copyleft licenses or cases involving human-modified AI code.

Tags: Copyright, Software, FOSS, AI, Law

* [Agents of Chaos](https://agentsofchaos.baulab.info/)
  
A two-week study deployed six autonomous LLM agents in a live Discord environment equipped with persistent memory, email, and shell access. Researchers identified ten significant security vulnerabilities and observed six emergent safety behaviors through continuous interaction. The resulting report provides case studies, raw data, and insights into patterns of multi-agent amplification and social coherence.

# Software Engineering, Data & Security 
* [Deciphering Glyph ::        What Is Code Review For?](https://blog.glyph.im/2026/03/what-is-code-review-for.html)
  
Code review serves poorly as a bug-catching mechanism due to human limitations like fatigue and oversight. Automated tools such as tests, linters, and security scanners are far more effective for detecting errors. Instead, the true value of human review lies in catching process failures, sharing team culture, and preventing stagnation, emphasizing its role as a fundamentally social activity. This logic extends to LLM-generated code, which requires rigorous automated quality checks rather than human review to ensure reliability.

Tags: Review, Process, Human, LLM, Culture

* [A Reminder That AI Grifters Are Also Selling Fake Video Game Guidebooks On Amazon](https://aftermath.site/video-game-guidebook-amazon/)
  
A growing influx of AI-generated video game guidebooks on Amazon preys on players seeking walkthroughs for popular or unreleased titles like *Grand Theft Auto VI*. These fake guides are frequently riddled with errors, plagiarized text, and AI-generated art. The trend is particularly concerning for children's games, leaving parents unknowingly purchasing unhelpful and unofficial manuals.
