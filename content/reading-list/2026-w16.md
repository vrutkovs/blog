# Tech Industry & Culture

* [Premium: The Hater's Guide to OpenAI](https://www.wheresyoured.at/hatersguide-openai/)

  A critical examination of OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman alleges a pattern of unsustainable financial practices, deep operating losses, and inflated revenue figures. Relying heavily on continuous venture capital and partner debt to fund massive compute commitments, the company is a highly leveraged structure rushing toward public markets despite internal concerns over its financial readiness.

  Tags: OpenAI, Altman, Finance, Deception, Criticism

* [The Pope Is Weak On Crime](https://aftermath.site/the-pope-is-weak-on-crime/)

  A satirical piece critiques a fictional "Pope Leo XIV" from a right-wing perspective, echoing conservative grievances often directed at political figures. By expressing sarcastic disappointment that the Pope does not align with hardline stances on crime, immigration, and the economy, the commentary pokes fun at the intersection of religious leadership and polarized political expectations.

  Tags: Pope, Satire, Politics, Religion, Opinion

* [That's Not What Unc Means](https://aftermath.site/unc-aave-african-american-vernacular-english-slang-gen-z/)

  Mainstream media frequently mischaracterizes African American Vernacular English (AAVE) terms, such as redefining "unc"—a term of respect for older community members—as Gen Z slang for out-of-touch individuals. This pattern reflects a broader issue of cultural appropriation where Black slang is adopted, stripped of its original context, and falsely attributed to new origins.

  Tags: AAVE, Slang, Culture, Media, Internet

* [One Billion Buildings](https://www.thetechbubble.info/p/one-billion-buildings)

  The tech boom's failure to generate a true megacity in the San Francisco Bay Area stems from structural economic differences rather than just restrictive housing policies. The modern tech sector's minimal labor requirements, high capital mobility, and the erosion of worker bargaining power have created a dispersed development pattern fundamentally different from past industrial eras.

  Tags: Technology, Urbanism, Capitalism, Labor, History

# Artificial Intelligence & Society

* [Premium: AI Isn't Too Big To Fail](https://www.wheresyoured.at/premium-ai-isnt-too-big-to-fail/)

  The economics of the AI industry remain fragile, characterized by heavy reliance on venture capital, stalled data center projects, and lukewarm secondary market interest. Unlike the 2008 financial crisis, the current AI bubble lacks massive securitization and systemic risk, meaning a potential collapse would impact specific markets without triggering a global economic catastrophe.

  Tags: AI, Bubble, Crisis, Finance, Investment

While Anthropic has partially refused some military demands, worker organizing remains the most viable path for challenging this complicity and driving ethical change across the industry.

  Tags: AI, Military, Trump, Ethics, Protest

* [The peril of laziness lost](https://bcantrill.dtrace.org/2026/04/12/the-peril-of-laziness-lost/)

  Larry Wall's classic programmer virtue of "laziness" represents the drive to create simple, powerful abstractions through deep intellectual work. Modern development workflows heavily reliant on LLMs often produce bloated and complex code because the tools lack this constraint-driven discipline, emphasizing the need for engineers to prioritize simplicity and use AI to enhance system design rather than merely boosting output volume.

  Tags: Programmer, Laziness, Abstraction, LLM, Design

* [Keep the Robots Out of the Gym](https://danielmiessler.com/blog/keep-the-robots-out-of-the-gym)

* [Cloudflare just launched EmDash, and its plugin sandbox is the real story](https://jorijn.com/en/blog/cloudflare-emdash-wordpress-plugin-security-model/)

While this architectural approach offers robust isolation, its reliance on Cloudflare's paid infrastructure introduces vendor lock-in, and its developer-focused ecosystem means it is not yet a direct WordPress replacement.

* [A Mac Studio for Local AI — 6 Months Later](https://spicyneuron.substack.com/p/a-mac-studio-for-local-ai-6-months)

  Optimizing a Mac Studio with 512GB of RAM enables running frontier-class large language models locally with high performance. By maximizing GPU memory, installing tools like `mlx-lm` and `claude-code-router`, and configuring software fixes for prompt caching, developers can build a robust local AI setup that bypasses rate limits and ensures privacy.

  Tags: AI, Mac, Model, Hardware, Software

* [WebP: The WebPage compression format](https://purplesyringa.moe/blog/webp-the-webpage-compression-format/)

  To bypass the lack of Brotli support on GitHub Pages, an experimental technique compressed HTML content into a lossless WebP image, decoding it client-side using JavaScript and the WebGL Canvas API. While it achieved significant file size reduction compared to gzip, challenges with loading flicker and browser anti-fingerprinting ultimately made the approach impractical for production.

  Tags: WebP, Compression, JavaScript, Browser, Performance

* [Installing every* Firefox extension](https://jack.cab/blog/every-firefox-extension)

  Scraping and installing all 84,194 available Firefox extensions in a single browser instance reveals insights into extension characteristics, permissions, and forms of abuse like phishing. Loading such a massive volume of extensions into a virtual machine unsurprisingly renders the browser largely unusable, resulting in severe performance degradation and frequent crashes.

  Tags: Extension, Firefox, Scraping, Malware, Performance
