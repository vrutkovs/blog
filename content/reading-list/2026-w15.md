# Artificial Intelligence & Society

* [The AI Great Leap Forward](https://leehanchung.github.io/blogs/2026/04/05/the-ai-great-leap-forward/)

  Corporate AI mandates are drawing comparisons to China's Great Leap Forward: companies build superficial solutions without expertise, generate inflated success metrics, and eliminate critical roles like middle management and QA based on AI promises. Employees protect themselves by creating hollow "anti-distillation" skills rather than sharing real expertise, producing an illusion of progress while accumulating future tech debt.

  Tags: AI, Mandate, Failure, Business, Culture

* [AI's aesthetics of failure](https://www.bloodinthemachine.com/p/ais-aesthetics-of-failure)

  OpenAI's Sora video app is shutting down after failing to attract users, largely due to its "slop aesthetics" — unpleasant, derivative, and often grotesque AI-generated content that triggered widespread revulsion. The failure highlights Silicon Valley's declining focus on design and user experience, and how AI slop visually embodies the goal of replicating and replacing human creative work.

  Tags: AI, Sora, Failure, Aesthetic, Slop

* [It's open season for refusing AI](https://www.bloodinthemachine.com/p/its-open-season-for-refusing-ai)

  A rising wave of AI refusals is taking hold across multiple sectors: nationwide moratoriums on data centers, Wikipedia banning AI-generated content, Capcom refusing AI assets, a major publisher canceling an AI-written novel. The movement marks a shift from backlash to direct refusal, driven by public skepticism, concerns about automation, labor exploitation, and environmental impact.

  Tags: AI, Refusal, Protest, Regulation, Labor

* [Anatomy of the .claude/ Folder](https://blog.dailydoseofds.com/p/anatomy-of-the-claude-folder)

The global `~/.claude/` stores personal preferences and session memory.

# Software Engineering & Cloud

* [The Comforting Lie Of SHA Pinning](https://www.vaines.org/posts/2026-03-24-the-comforting-lie-of-sha-pinning/)

  Pinning a GitHub Action to a commit SHA doesn't guarantee the action's origin. GitHub's commit resolution allows malicious code from an attacker's fork to execute by simply updating the SHA in a pull request — even with the repository reference unchanged — bypassing organizational restrictions and making standard review practices inadequate against supply chain attacks.

  Tags: Security, SupplyChain, GitHub, Action, Pinning

* [Shell Tricks That Actually Make Life Easier (And Save Your Sanity)](https://blog.hofstede.it/shell-tricks-that-actually-make-life-easier-and-save-your-sanity/)

  Tags: Shell, Unix, Terminal, Command, Script

* [The difficulty of making sure your website is broken](https://letsencrypt.org/2026/04/10/test-sites.html)

  Have you ever needed to make sure your website has a broken certificate? While many tools exist to help run an HTTPS server with valid certificates, there aren't tools to make sure your certificate is revoked or expired. This is not a problem most people have. Tools to help manage certificates are always focused on avoiding those problems, not creating them.Let's Encrypt is a Certificate Authority, and so we have unusual problems we need to solve.

  Tags: Certificate, Revocation, Testing, HTTPS, ACME

* [Factorio: Recycler Belt Stacking](https://blog.ktz.me/factorio-recycler-belt-stacking/)
