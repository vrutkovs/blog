# Software Engineering, Data & Security 
* [Open Source Gave Me Everything Until I Had Nothing Left to Give](https://kennethreitz.org/essays/2026-03-18-open_source_gave_me_everything_until_i_had_nothing_left_to_give)
  
Kenneth Reitz recounts how his identity became deeply intertwined with his prolific open-source work on the Requests library, leading to psychiatric emergencies due to undiagnosed bipolar disorder and an unsustainable lifestyle. Detailing the immense personal cost of burnout and mental health crises, he advises fellow maintainers to separate their identity from projects, seek medical help, and establish strict boundaries to prioritize personal well-being over code contributions.

Tags: MentalHealth, Identity, Maintainer, Crisis, Sacrifice

* [The MacBook Neo](https://daringfireball.net/2026/03/the_macbook_neo)
  
A review of the hypothetical MacBook Neo explores a $600 laptop powered by an iPhone's A18 Pro chip. The device offers exceptional performance, display quality, and build quality, easily surpassing budget x86 PCs despite minor compromises like the lack of an ambient light sensor. The Neo serves as a highly practical secondary device, signaling a shift in Apple's design philosophy towards broader mass-market appeal.

Tags: MacBook, Apple, Chip, Cost, Laptop

* [Deep vs Shallow Go interfaces](https://tpaschalis.me/shallow-vs-deep-interfaces/)
  
An exploration of deep versus shallow Go interfaces draws on concepts from John Ousterhout's *A Philosophy of Software Design*. The comparison highlights how deeper interfaces conceal complexity and provide robust functionality, whereas shallow interfaces expose too much implementation detail and increase cognitive load for developers.

Tags: Go, Interface, Design, Pattern, Software

* [The power of single-method interfaces in Go - Eli Benderskys website](https://eli.thegreenplace.net/2023/the-power-of-single-method-interfaces-in-go/)
  
Single-method interfaces in Go offer a powerful and highly generalizable approach to software design. By defining small, focused abstractions like `io.Reader` or `io.Writer`, developers can create more composable, testable, and loosely coupled codebases.

Tags: Go, Interface, Design, Pattern, Software
